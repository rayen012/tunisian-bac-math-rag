\subsection{Summary}
\label{subsec:summary}

This thesis presented the design, implementation, and evaluation of a
Retrieval-Augmented Generation system for the Tunisian Baccalaureate
mathematics examination. We developed a complete pipeline from raw document
digitization through vector indexing to interactive answer generation, and
compared three approaches: a RAG system grounded in retrieved curriculum
documents, a prompt-only baseline relying on encoded syllabus knowledge, and
a hybrid engine that dynamically routes between the two based on retrieval
confidence.

The system addresses a practical gap in educational technology for Tunisian
secondary school students, providing the first AI tutoring tool specifically
designed for the Tunisian Bac mathematics curriculum and its distinctive
correction style.

\subsection{Future Work}
\label{subsec:future-work}

Several directions for future work emerge from this thesis:

\begin{enumerate}
    \item \textbf{User evaluation}: Conducting a study with actual Tunisian
    Bac students to assess the system's pedagogical effectiveness in
    practice.

    \item \textbf{Automated evaluation}: Developing automated metrics for
    mathematical correctness and style adherence, potentially using LLM-as-judge
    approaches.

    \item \textbf{Corpus expansion}: Extending the corpus to cover all
    Baccalaureate sections (Sciences, Technique, \'{E}conomie) and additional
    years of past examinations.

    \item \textbf{Fine-tuning}: Exploring fine-tuning of the embedding model
    on Tunisian Bac mathematical content to improve retrieval quality for
    domain-specific terminology.

    \item \textbf{Interactive features}: Adding step-by-step guided problem
    solving, where the system reveals the solution incrementally rather than
    presenting the full correction at once.
\end{enumerate}
