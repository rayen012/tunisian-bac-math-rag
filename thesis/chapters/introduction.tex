\chapter{Introduction}

\section{Motivation}
In the Tunisian educational landscape, the Baccalaureate examination represents a critical socio-economic milestone. For decades, the high-stakes nature of this exam has fueled a massive "shadow education" system, where private tutoring is viewed as an essential requirement for success.Tunisian households invest a lot of money monthly per student in private instruction. Despite this heavy financial burden, traditional tutoring remains limited by logistical constraints, such as large group sizes and restricted weekly availability. Furthermore, many students face psychological barriers, hesitating to address fundamental conceptual gaps due to the social friction inherent in group-based learning.

The emergence of Large Language Models (LLMs) offers a potential democratization of this specialized support. In global contexts, LLMs provide 24/7  access to personalized learning, allowing students to resolve queries without social anxiety. However, for the Tunisian context, general-purpose models like (ChatGPT , gemini.... ) frequently prove insufficiency . These models often provide solutions that are  not  from the official Tunisian Ministry of Education curriculum, utilizing foreign methodologies or unconventional mathematical formalisms (redaction) that can lead to point deductions during official grading.

\section{Problem Statement}
While current state-of-the-art multimodal LLMs provide impressive general reasoning, they lack "curriculum-alignment" for localized national exams. In the domain of Mathematics, the specific logic and redaction style required in Tunisian conventions are not fully captured by base models. Consequently, students attempting to use AI for revision face a high risk of "hallucination" regarding regional pedagogical standards. There is a clear research gap in creating a specialized AI agent that combines the reasoning power of modern LLMs with a strictly grounded knowledge base of official Tunisian exam archives and methodologies.

\section{Research Objectives}
This thesis aims to bridge the gap between global AI capabilities and local educational requirements. We evaluate different augmentation strategies, primarily Retrieval-Augmented Generation (RAG), to develop an AI-based Mathematics tutor tailored for the Tunisian Baccalaureate (Mathematics section). By indexing a specialized dataset of official exams, handwritten corrections, and textbook materials, we aim to provide a system that adheres to Tunisian "redaction" standards while offering the personalized support typical of a high-level private tutor.

\section{Thesis Structure}

The structure  of this thesis is organized as follows. Chapter 2 reviews the
background and related work relevant to large language models, retrieval-
augmented generation, prompt engineering, hybrid architectures, and AI in
mathematics education. Chapter 3 describes the methodology and implementation
of the three systems developed in this thesis: the RAG system, the
prompt-only baseline, and the hybrid engine. Chapter 4 presents the
evaluation results. Chapter 5 discusses these findings and their
implications, and Chapter 6 concludes the thesis.
