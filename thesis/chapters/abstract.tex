This thesis presents the design, implementation, and evaluation of a
Retrieval-Augmented Generation (RAG) system for the Tunisian Baccalaureate
mathematics examination (Section Math\'{e}matiques). The system combines a
curated corpus of digitized course material, exercise series, and corrected
past examinations with a two-stage retrieval mechanism and a large language
model to produce curriculum-faithful answers that mimic the official Tunisian
correction style.

We compare three approaches: (1)~a pure RAG system that grounds every answer
in retrieved documents, (2)~a prompt-only baseline that encodes the entire
syllabus into the system prompt without retrieval, and (3)~a hybrid engine
that routes queries to RAG or prompt-only mode based on retrieval confidence.

The system is deployed as an interactive web application and addresses a
practical gap in educational technology for the Tunisian secondary school
context, where no domain-specific AI tutoring tool previously existed.
